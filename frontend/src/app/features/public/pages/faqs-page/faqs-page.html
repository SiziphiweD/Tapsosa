FAQs

Answers to common questions about onboarding, bidding, compliance, and escrow-backed payments on the UKHUSELO SUPPLIER PORTAL platform.

Getting Started

Who can register as a member?

Members are organisations that procure security or related services and products. You register your company profile, contact details, and basic verification information before posting requests.

Who can register as a supplier?

Suppliers are vetted service providers and product vendors in categories such as guarding, uniforms and PPE, technology, patrol monitoring, radios, finance, and insurance.

Requests & Bidding

How do members submit requirements?

Members complete a structured request form that captures category, scope, quantities, location, delivery timelines, and budget range. Once submitted, the request is published to eligible suppliers.

How do suppliers receive job alerts?

Suppliers are notified in real time when new requests match their registered categories, allowing them to review the brief and submit bids directly on the platform.

Bid Evaluation

How are bids compared?

Bids are presented in a standardised comparison view showing pricing, specifications, timelines, and compliance information so members can make like-for-like decisions.

Can members see supplier history?

Where available, the platform surfaces past transaction history, performance indicators, and compliance status to support informed selection.

Compliance & Verification

What compliance checks are performed?

TAPSOSA verifies supplier credentials, regulatory documents, and other compliance requirements, and confirms that members are in good standing before trade is activated.

What happens when documents expire?

Expiry alerts and trade controls help ensure that suppliers maintain up-to-date documentation in order to remain eligible for new work.

Payments & Fees

How does escrow work?

Members fund payments into escrow via the integrated gateway. Funds are held until delivery or milestones are confirmed, then released to the supplier.
